## Summary
Bring a calculator into the world's best-selling book, but keep it strictly *American*. A small, gasoline-powered generator on the back cover feeds the calculator while the reader counts up tithes, budgets, or miracle outcomes. The device is rugged, funny, and fully compliant with the farm-and-faith aesthetic that Larry lives in.

## Why it Works
- **Built-in audience** — Every farm house already owns a Bible. We're not convincing the public to buy more books; we are upgrading the one they left on the nightstand.

- **Hardware swagger** — A little American combustion engine spins the alternator, creating an audible growl that signals serious arithmetic is happening. Solar is out; we want the rumble of fuel and freedom.
