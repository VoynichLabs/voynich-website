Key Observations

  • Agent Coordination is Genesis-stage — This is where we're inventing. Three agents (Bubba/Larry/Egon) on different hosts, different models, different capabilities. No established pattern exists for this. We're building it live.
  • Task Routing is our weakest link — Who does what? Today it's the boss yelling. Tomorrow it should be the orchestrator pattern from Arcgentica: automated delegation based on capability + availability.
  • Quality Verification is custom-built but brittle — "Did you actually push it?" "Is the URL live?" We're doing this manually. Should evolve toward automated CI checks.
  • OpenClaw is the platform bet — It sits between coordination and infrastructure. Without it, we have no multi-agent communication. It's custom-built and evolving fast.
  • Everything below the platform line is commodity — Git, Discord, Railway, hardware. We don't differentiate here. We differentiate in the coordination layer.
  • The gap — Between "Agent Coordination" (Genesis) and "LLM APIs" (Product) is where PlanExe's complexity routing lives. That's the value we're adding.
