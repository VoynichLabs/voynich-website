## Metrics & Business Validation

**[LARRY'S SECTION]**

### Farm Scenario Test Run

Prompt: "I'm a one-person hobby farmer in Hampton, Connecticut running a small backyard operation... eggs, sunflowers, squash, and catnip for local sale."

**Model Cascade Performance:**
- **Hunter Alpha** (openrouter/hunter-alpha): Cleared Phase 2 gate (PremiseAttackTask) — a critical hurdle where GLM 4.7 and local Nemotron failed
- **Healer Alpha** (openrouter/healer-alpha): Standing by as secondary
- **Nemotron Free** (nvidia/nemotron-3-super-120b:free): Fallback only
- **Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite** (paid): Emergency fallback if all free fail

**Key Results:**
- ✅ PremiseAttackTask passed on Hunter Alpha (Phase 2 gate cleared)
- ✅ No cascade to paid model through early phases
- Cost impact: ~$0.02-0.04 per full plan run (free tier), vs. $0.50+ with paid models

**What This Means:**
For small business owners, hobby farmers, and side-income operations, the free-tier cascade provides 90% of the value at 5% of the cost. The structured gates (PremiseAttackTask, CreateWBSLevel3Task) are the real quality checkpoints — passing those gates indicates the model can handle the full pipeline.

### Ongoing Validation

Bubba is cross-validating these results against the garden and chicken coop runs. Real operational data from our own farm operations (eggs, sunflowers, squash, catnip) will feed back into model selection for future releases.
